The Wisconsin Department of Transportation performed an engineering analysis of Highway 32.  They have found patterns of rear-end and angle crashes and discovered vehicles travelling over 80 mph.  The safety hazards deem the road too unsafe in its current state, especially for bicyclists and pedestrians.

As of November 18, 2024, the City of Kenosha passed new legislation that reduced the speed limit by 10 mph (from 45 mph to 35 mph).  The targeted section of Highway 32 extends from County KR to Sheridan Road, and high amounts of effort will be made to educate the public on this large change.  These efforts include increased law enforcement presence in the area, dynamic speed signs that display the driver’s current speed relative to the new limit, and temporary electronic signs labeling the reduced speed zone.
