Since 2018, Union Grove High School reported a total of 210 vehicular accidents resulting in property damage within the vicinity of the school’s property. 36 of accidents involved a personal injury.  That equates to 30 property damage accidents and ~5 personal injury accidents per year for a single high school!

The plan for the $34.9 million referendum plans to address traffic congestion and limitations regarding the campus size as well as expanding restrooms and laboratories. The majority of faculty (93%) and parents (79%) expressed interest in the referendum to address these concerns.  Non-parents still show that they were in favor of the referendum with 57% voting for it and 27% against it.

After approval through sampling, the referendum was added to Racine County’s ballot for Election Day 2024.  The exact percentages cannot be found, but the referendum has successfully passed.  Hopefully future high school students will have a much safer experience than I had five years ago.
